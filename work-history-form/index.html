Recruiters will evaluate your career path, looking at how long you’ve held each job, any promotions, whether you’ve worked in similar industries, and if there are any employment gaps.

  • Provide the basic details of your previous positions so employers can see your work experience.
  • Don’t shorten job titles — using the full title appears more professional and is easier for hiring managers to understand. Include the start and end dates for each position.
  • If dates are missing, employers may suspect you’re hiding something.
  • Not sure of the exact start date or job title? No problem — input your best guess and revise it later once you have accurate information.
