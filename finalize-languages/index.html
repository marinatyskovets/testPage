Expert Insights
When adding languages to your resume, don't forget to specify your skill level.
  • Beginner (A1): Can understand basic phrases, introduce themselves, and ask or answer personal questions if the conversation is slow and clear.
  • Elementary (A2): Can understand everyday sentences on topics like family and shopping. Can do basic tasks needing direct communication. Can describe simple things about their life and needs.
  • Intermediate (B1): Understands basic information in work and school. Can manage travel situations in places with that language. Can write simply on known subjects and describe hopes and experiences
  • Upper Intermediate (B2): Understands key points in complicated texts, including technical ones. Can chat easily with native speakers. Can write clearly about many topics and discuss the pros and cons of issues.
  • Advanced (C1): Can read difficult texts and see hidden meanings. Can talk smoothly and naturally. Can use language well in different settings. Can write clear and detailed pieces on hard subjects.
  • Proficient (C2): Easily understands everything they hear or read. Can summarize info from various sources and present it clearly. Speaks fluently and precisely, even in complex situations.
